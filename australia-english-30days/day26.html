1 Reading

キッチンで使う英語

Kitchen English

Working in a kitchen requires special vocabulary. You need to know words for equipment like stove, oven, grill, and fryer. Action words are important too: chop, dice, slice, fry, boil, steam, and grill. In a busy kitchen, communication must be fast and clear. Chefs say 'behind!' when walking behind someone, and 'hot!' when carrying something hot. 'Yes, chef!' shows you understood an instruction.

3 Review
📍 My First Day in the Kitchen
⭐⭐⭐⭐

My first day in the Australian kitchen was intense! The head chef spoke fast and I didn't understand everything. But I learned quickly. When he says 'Check!' I know the order is ready. When I hear 'Fire!' it means start cooking that order. 'All day' means the total number. I made some mistakes but everyone was patient. Tomorrow will be better!
