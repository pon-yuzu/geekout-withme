Understanding Australian Time Zones

Australia is a country that has three main time zones. The eastern states, which include New South Wales, Victoria, and Queensland, use Australian Eastern Standard Time. This zone is one hour ahead of Japan during winter. Central Australia, which covers South Australia and the Northern Territory, is 30 minutes behind the east. Western Australia uses a time zone that is one hour behind Japan. To make things more complicated, daylight saving time has been adopted by some states but not others. Queensland, Western Australia, and the Northern Territory have chosen not to use it. This means that during summer, the time difference between Sydney and Tokyo changes from one hour to two hours. Many working holiday makers who have moved to Australia find it confusing at first when scheduling video calls with their family back in Japan.

📍 Sydney Opera House
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

The Sydney Opera House is one of the most famous buildings in the world, which was designed by Danish architect Jorn Utzon. It was completed in 1973 after a construction process that had taken 16 years. The building has been recognised as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered a masterpiece of modern architecture. I have taken a guided tour inside, and I was deeply impressed by the concert halls that were built with incredible attention to detail. The view from the harbour, which includes both the Opera House and the Sydney Harbour Bridge, has become one of the most photographed scenes in Australia. I have visited at different times of day, and the building looks completely different depending on the lighting. Evening visits are especially recommended because the sails of the building are beautifully illuminated. It is a must-see destination that should not be missed.

5 🦘 Australia Tips

3つのタイムゾーンを覚えよう

東部（AEST）: 日本+1時間、中部（ACST）: 日本+30分、西部（AWST）: 日本-1時間。ただしサマータイム期間（10月〜4月頃）は東部と中部がさらに1時間進みます。西部はサマータイムを使いません。

日本との連絡のコツ

東部にいる場合、日本の夜8時はオーストラリアの夜9時（冬）または夜10時（夏）です。家族や友人とのビデオ通話は、日本の夜・オーストラリアの夜という時間帯が合わせやすいです。World Clock アプリを入れておくと便利です。

州をまたぐ移動に注意

州をまたいで移動すると時間が変わることがあります。特にQLD州とNSW州の州境では、サマータイム期間中に1時間の差が生まれます。バスや電車の時刻表は現地時間で表示されるので、移動前に必ず確認しましょう。

B:
Well, Sydney is one hour ahead of Japan right now. So if it's 8 PM here, it's 7 PM in Tokyo.
A (Koa):
Oh, that's not a big difference. I thought the time difference was larger.
B:
It's not too bad. But it changes when daylight saving has been activated in summer.
A (Koa):
How much does it change?
B:
During daylight saving, we're two hours ahead of Japan. So our 8 PM becomes 6 PM in Tokyo.
A (Koa):
That's still manageable. By the way, I've heard that not all states use daylight saving.
B:
That's correct. Queensland, which is right next to us, doesn't use it. It gets quite confusing!
A (Koa):
I can imagine! I'll download a world clock app that has been recommended by other backpackers.
B:
Good idea! That will make it much easier to keep track of the time difference.
