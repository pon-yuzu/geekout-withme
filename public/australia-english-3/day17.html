多文化の食

A World of Food in Australia

Australia is a country that has been shaped by immigration from all over the world. This diversity is clearly seen in its food culture. In any major Australian city, you can find restaurants that serve food from dozens of different countries. Vietnamese pho, which was introduced by refugees in the 1970s, has become one of the most popular dishes in Australia. Greek, Italian, and Lebanese food, which have been enjoyed for decades, are considered part of everyday Australian cuisine. Food markets, which are held in many cities, offer a wonderful chance to try dishes from around the world. The Australian food scene has been enriched by people who have brought their traditions with them. This multicultural approach to food is something that Australians are very proud of.

3 Review
📍 Queen Victoria Market — Melbourne
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Queen Victoria Market is a place that every food lover should visit when they come to Melbourne. This market, which has been operating since 1878, is one of the largest open-air markets in the world. I have visited this market many times, and I am always amazed by the variety of food that is sold here. Fresh vegetables, meat, seafood, and cheese are all available at reasonable prices. The food court section, which was recently renovated, offers meals from over twenty different countries. I tried a Turkish gözleme that was made right in front of me, and it was fantastic. The market is also known for its night market events, which are held during summer. If you haven't been here yet, you are missing out on a truly special experience.

5 🦘 Australia Tips

多文化のオーストラリア

オーストラリアの人口の約30%は海外生まれです。そのため "Where are you originally from?"（もともとどこの出身？）は普通の会話です。食べ物の話は文化交流のきっかけになります。

マーケットでの買い物フレーズ

マーケットでは "Can I try a sample?"（試食できますか？）や "How much is this per kilo?"（1キロいくらですか？）が便利です。値切りは普通ではありませんが、大量購入なら "Any discount for buying more?" と聞いてもOKです。

That's the best problem to have! Let's get a bit of everything and share.
