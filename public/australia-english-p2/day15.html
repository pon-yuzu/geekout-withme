オーストラリアのカフェ文化

How Australia Shaped Global Coffee Culture

Had it not been for the wave of Italian and Greek immigrants arriving after World War II, Australia's café culture might never have developed into what it is today. These immigrants, longing for the espresso traditions of their homelands, established small cafés that would eventually transform how an entire nation consumed coffee. The flat white, believed to have been invented in Australia during the 1980s, has since gained international recognition. What makes Australian café culture particularly distinctive is the emphasis placed on quality over convenience. Were you to walk into a typical Australian café, you would find single-origin beans roasted on-site and baristas trained to an exceptionally high standard. Major chain coffee shops, having attempted to enter the Australian market, largely failed because consumers preferred artisanal alternatives. Melbourne, often referred to as the coffee capital of the world, boasts more independent cafés per capita than almost any other city. Not only has this culture influenced neighbouring countries, but it has also reshaped expectations worldwide.

3 Review
📍 Proud Mary Coffee — Melbourne
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

If I were asked to recommend just one café in Melbourne, it would undoubtedly be Proud Mary. Situated in the vibrant suburb of Collingwood, this establishment has earned a reputation that extends far beyond Australia's borders. The flat white, prepared using beans sourced from sustainable farms and roasted in their own facility, is nothing short of extraordinary. What struck me most was the meticulous attention paid to every aspect of the experience, from the carefully curated menu to the industrial-chic interior design. The baristas, having undergone rigorous training, demonstrated an impressive depth of knowledge about each coffee's origin and flavour profile. Had I visited earlier in the day, I would have tried their renowned brunch menu, which is said to feature seasonal ingredients from local producers. Not once during my visit did the service feel rushed or impersonal. Were I to return to Melbourne, Proud Mary would be the first place I visited.

5 🦘 Australia Tips

オーストラリアのコーヒー注文表現

オーストラリアでは "Can I grab a flat white?" や "I'll have a long black, thanks" のようにカジュアルに注文します。grab は「買う・もらう」の口語表現です。サイズは small / regular / large が一般的で、アメリカのような tall / grande は通じません。takeaway（持ち帰り）も重要な単語です。

仮定法倒置で洗練された表現を

"Had it not been for..." は "If it had not been for..." の倒置形で、フォーマルな文章でよく使われます。同様に "Were you to visit..." は "If you were to visit..." の倒置です。英検準2級ではこうした倒置構文の読解力が求められます。

6 Conversation
🎭 友達にメルボルンのカフェ文化について教えてもらう
A (Koa):
I've heard that Melbourne is famous for its coffee. Is that really true?
B:
Absolutely. If you were to visit any neighbourhood in Melbourne, you'd find at least five or six independent cafés.
A (Koa):
That's incredible! I was told that Starbucks failed in Australia. Is that accurate?
B:
It is, actually. Having tried to compete with local baristas, they couldn't match the quality Australians expected.
A (Koa):
What would you recommend I order if I went to a café here?
B:
I'd suggest you try a flat white. It's what most Australians consider to be the perfect coffee.
A (Koa):
Someone mentioned something called a long black. What's that?
B:
It's similar to an Americano but made differently. The espresso is poured over hot water, which preserves the crema on top.
A (Koa):
Interesting! Were I to become a coffee lover, this would be the perfect city.
B:
Without a doubt! Not only would you discover amazing coffee, but you'd also experience the social side of café culture.
A (Koa):
What do you mean by the social side?
B:
Cafés here are where people catch up, work remotely, and build community. It's much more than just a drink.
