Navigating the Working Holiday Visa Application

Were it not for the Working Holiday visa programme, thousands of young travellers would never have the opportunity to immerse themselves in Australian culture. The subclass 417 visa, which permits holders to reside and work in Australia for up to twelve months, is available to applicants aged between eighteen and thirty. It is essential that the applicant possess a valid passport, sufficient funds to support themselves initially, and adequate health insurance. Had the Australian government not introduced this scheme in the 1970s, the cultural exchange between nations would have been significantly diminished. The application, submitted exclusively online, requires a fee of approximately 640 Australian dollars. Processing times, which vary considerably depending on nationality, may range from several days to several weeks. Applicants are advised to commence preparations well in advance, ensuring that all documentation, including medical examinations where applicable, has been completed prior to submission. Once granted, the visa holder is permitted to undertake employment with any single employer for a maximum of six months.

3 Review
📍 Australian Immigration Advisory Centre, Sydney CBD
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Having struggled with the visa application process for weeks, I decided to visit this advisory centre, which turned out to be the best decision I could have made. The consultant, a woman who had herself worked in the immigration department for over a decade, explained everything so thoroughly that even the most complex regulations became comprehensible. She suggested that I submit my health examination results before lodging the application, which I wish someone had told me earlier. Not only did she clarify the documentation requirements, but she also provided invaluable advice regarding the second-year visa extension. Had I attempted to navigate the process alone, I would undoubtedly have made costly errors. The centre, conveniently situated near Wynyard Station, offers appointments in multiple languages, a service that is particularly appreciated by non-native English speakers. I would strongly recommend this place to anyone contemplating a Working Holiday in Australia.

5 🦘 Australia Tips

仮定法で丁寧さを表現しよう

オーストラリアの公的機関では 'I would appreciate it if you could...' や 'Were there any issues, please contact us' のような仮定法表現が多用されます。直接的な命令文よりも丁寧な印象を与えるため、ビザ関連の問い合わせメールでは特に有効です。

オージー英語の 'lodge' に注意

オーストラリアでは申請書を「提出する」ことを 'lodge an application' と言います。イギリス英語の 'submit' やアメリカ英語の 'file' と同じ意味ですが、ビザや税金関連の書類では 'lodge' が圧倒的に多く使われます。

🎭 移民エージェントのオフィスでビザについて相談する場面
A (Koa):
G'day! How can I help you today?
B:
I'd like to enquire about the Working Holiday visa. If I were to apply now, how long would the processing take?
A (Koa):
Well, it depends on your nationality. Were you to apply as a Japanese citizen, it'd typically take about two to three weeks.
B:
I see. I've heard that there are certain requirements regarding financial evidence. Could you elaborate on that?
A (Koa):
Certainly. It's required that you demonstrate access to at least 5,000 Australian dollars, in addition to a return airfare.
B:
Had I known about that earlier, I would have started saving sooner. What about the health examination?
A (Koa):
Not everyone needs one, but should the department request it, you'd need to visit an approved panel physician.
B:
That's very helpful. I was also wondering whether the second-year visa requires specific regional work.
A (Koa):
Absolutely. You'd need to complete 88 days of specified work in a designated regional area, which includes farming, mining, and construction.
B:
If I were to do farm work in Queensland, would that qualify?
A (Koa):
It certainly would. Queensland is one of the most popular regions for that purpose. Many backpackers who have completed their farm work there reported positive experiences.
B:
Brilliant. I really appreciate your assistance. I'll commence the application process this week.
