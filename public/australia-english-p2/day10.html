Brisbane: The Sunshine State Capital

Brisbane, the capital of Queensland — often referred to as the Sunshine State — has undergone a remarkable transformation over the past two decades. What was once dismissed as a sleepy, provincial city has evolved into a vibrant cultural hub that rivals Sydney and Melbourne in many respects. Had you visited Brisbane in the early 2000s, you would scarcely have recognised the city that exists today. The South Bank Parklands, built on the former site of World Expo 88, feature an artificial beach, lush rainforest gardens, and a network of riverside promenades that attract millions of visitors annually. Brisbane's subtropical climate, characterised by warm winters and hot, humid summers, means that outdoor living is not merely a preference but a way of life. It is reported that Brisbane receives approximately three hundred days of sunshine per year, which explains why residents are known for their relaxed, optimistic disposition. The city's successful bid to host the 2032 Olympic Games has further accelerated its development, with billions of dollars being invested in infrastructure and cultural facilities.

3 Review
📍 South Bank Parklands & Streets Beach
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

If someone had told me that one of Australia's best beaches was located in the middle of a city, I would not have believed them — yet here it is. Streets Beach at South Bank is a stunning artificial lagoon surrounded by white sand and tropical plants, right on the banks of the Brisbane River. It is said that this is the only inner-city man-made beach in Australia, and having experienced it firsthand, I can understand why locals are so proud of it. The water is crystal clear and maintained to the highest standards, with lifeguards on duty throughout the day. What impressed me most was how families, joggers, and office workers all shared this space harmoniously. Adjacent to the beach, the cultural precinct houses the Queensland Art Gallery, the Gallery of Modern Art, and the Queensland Museum — all of which offer free admission. Were I to recommend a single destination that captures Brisbane's spirit of outdoor living and cultural sophistication, South Bank would be my unequivocal choice.

5 🦘 Australia Tips

Brekkie, Arvo, Barbie — オージースラング入門

オーストラリア人は単語を短縮するのが大好きです。breakfast → brekkie（朝食）、afternoon → arvo（午後）、barbecue → barbie（バーベキュー）。ブリスベンでは天気がいいので 'Let's have a barbie this arvo!'（今日の午後バーベキューしよう！）とよく聞きます。

受動態の報告表現 — It is reported that...

'It is reported that...'（〜と報告されている）は客観的な情報を伝える便利な表現です。同様に 'It is said that...'（〜と言われている）、'It is believed that...'（〜と信じられている）なども使えます。準2級のリーディングでよく出題される形式です。

🎭 ブリスベンのサウスバンクで地元の人と知り合い、おすすめスポットを聞く
A (Koa):
Excuse me, is it all right if I sit here? The other benches are all taken.
B:
Yeah, no worries! Take a seat. Are you visiting Brisbane?
A (Koa):
Yes, I arrived yesterday. I'm amazed by how much sunshine there is here.
B:
Ha! That's why it's called the Sunshine State. If you were to live here for a while, you'd get used to it pretty quickly.
A (Koa):
I was told that Brisbane used to be a quiet city. Is it true that it's changed a lot?
B:
Absolutely. Had you come here twenty years ago, you wouldn't recognise the place. The Olympics bid really transformed everything.
A (Koa):
Speaking of which, are the locals excited about the 2032 Olympics?
B:
Most people are, yeah. Though some worry about the cost. If the budget were managed properly, it would be brilliant for the city.
A (Koa):
I see. Could you recommend any good spots for dinner around here?
B:
If I were in your shoes, I'd head to Eat Street Northshore. It's a shipping container market with food from all over the world.
A (Koa):
That sounds incredible. How do I get there from here?
B:
Catch the ferry from South Bank terminal — it's about a thirty-minute ride. You won't regret it!
