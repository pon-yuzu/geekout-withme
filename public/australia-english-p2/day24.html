Understanding Your Tax File Number

A Tax File Number, commonly abbreviated to TFN, is a unique identifier issued by the Australian Taxation Office, without which no individual can legally be employed in Australia. It is imperative that every Working Holiday maker apply for a TFN promptly upon arrival, as failure to do so would result in the highest marginal tax rate being applied to their earnings. The application, which must be submitted through the ATO's official website, requires the applicant to have entered Australia on a valid visa. Had the system not been digitalised in recent years, applicants would have been obliged to attend an office in person, a process that was considerably more time-consuming. Once the application has been lodged, the TFN is typically issued within twenty-eight days, delivered via post to the applicant's registered address. It is strongly recommended that this number be memorised or securely stored, given that it will be required by every employer, financial institution, and superannuation fund throughout the individual's time in Australia.

3 Review
📍 ATO Online Application Portal
⭐⭐⭐⭐

Applying for my TFN online was remarkably straightforward, though I wish the website had provided clearer instructions for those whose English is not their first language. The entire process, which took no longer than fifteen minutes, required only my passport details, visa information, and Australian postal address. I was informed that the TFN would arrive within four weeks; however, mine was delivered in just eighteen days, which exceeded my expectations. What I found particularly useful was the confirmation email, containing a reference number that could be used to track the application's progress. It should be mentioned that, had I not had a permanent address at the time of application, I could have used a friend's address or even a hostel, provided that mail could be received there. One minor criticism would be that nowhere on the website was it clearly stated that the TFN should never be shared with anyone other than official entities. Overall, navigating the portal was considerably easier than I had anticipated.

5 🦘 Australia Tips

倒置構文でフォーマル度アップ

'Nowhere on the website was it stated...' のように、否定語を文頭に置く倒置構文は、フォーマルな文章やレビューでよく使われます。'Never have I seen...', 'Seldom does this happen...' なども同じパターンです。準2級では頻出の構文です。

TFNとスーパーの関係

オーストラリアでは雇用主が従業員の給料の一部を年金（superannuation、通称 'super'）として積み立てる義務があります。TFNがないとスーパーの口座も開けません。'What's your super fund?' は職場でよく聞かれる質問です。

B:
That's outrageous! I wish someone had told me about this before I left Japan.
A (Koa):
I know, right? But the good news is that the application process is entirely online and takes only about fifteen minutes.
B:
That's reassuring. What information would I need to have on hand?
A (Koa):
Your passport details, your visa grant number, and an Australian address where they can post the TFN. You could even use this hostel's address.
B:
Really? I didn't think that would be permitted. Do you know how long it takes to receive it?
A (Koa):
The ATO states that it should arrive within twenty-eight days, although mine came in less than three weeks.
B:
Excellent. And once I receive it, should I carry the letter with me at all times?
A (Koa):
I'd recommend memorising the number and storing the letter somewhere secure. Under no circumstances should you share it with anyone other than your employer or your bank.
B:
Understood. I'll apply straightaway. I really appreciate you explaining all of this to me.
