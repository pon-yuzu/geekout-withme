Vegemite: Australia's Most Divisive Spread

No discussion of Australian food culture would be complete without addressing Vegemite — the dark, intensely savoury yeast extract spread that has simultaneously delighted and horrified people around the world. Developed in 1922 by a chemist named Cyril Callister, who had been tasked with creating a spread from leftover brewer's yeast, Vegemite has since become one of Australia's most recognisable cultural exports. It is estimated that approximately twenty-two million jars are sold domestically each year, a figure suggesting that scarcely a household in the country exists without one. The key to appreciating Vegemite, seasoned consumers insist, lies in understanding how it should be applied. Were a newcomer to spread it thickly like peanut butter or jam, as many international visitors mistakenly do, the overwhelming saltiness would render the experience decidedly unpleasant. Instead, a thin scraping on buttered toast is the method universally recommended by Australians. Rich in B vitamins and possessing a distinctive umami flavour, Vegemite occupies a position in Australian identity that transcends mere gastronomy — it is, for many, an emotionally charged symbol of home itself.

3 Review
📍 Vegemite Tasting Experience — Federation Square, Melbourne
⭐⭐⭐⭐

If someone had told me before I arrived in Australia that I would voluntarily attend a tasting session dedicated entirely to a yeast-based spread, I would have questioned their sanity. Yet there I was at Federation Square, participating in what turned out to be one of the most culturally illuminating experiences of my entire trip. The session, conducted by a passionate food historian who appeared to regard Vegemite with an almost reverential devotion, guided us through the product's century-long history. Participants were instructed to taste Vegemite in progressively increasing quantities, beginning with the thinnest possible layer on buttered toast. Had the organisers not structured the tasting so carefully, I suspect most international visitors would have abandoned the experience after the first bite. What I found most fascinating was the emotional response it provoked among Australian participants — several confessed that the taste transported them back to childhood mornings, evoking memories they had not revisited in years. Rarely had I witnessed food function so powerfully as a vehicle for nostalgia. Were I to recommend one uniquely Australian experience to a visitor, this would be it.

5 🦘 Australia Tips

ベジマイトの正しい食べ方

ベジマイトの鉄則は「薄く塗る」こと。トーストにバターを塗り、その上にナイフの先でうっすらとベジマイトをのせます。"Just a scraping"（こすりつける程度）がポイントです。オーストラリア人が "You're using too much!" と言ったら、量を減らしましょう。

仮定法を使った感想の表現

"If someone had told me I would enjoy Vegemite, I wouldn't have believed them."（ベジマイトを楽しめると言われていたら、信じなかっただろう）のように、仮定法過去完了は予想外の体験を振り返るときに便利です。"I would have questioned their sanity"（正気を疑っただろう）はユーモラスな強調表現です。

🎭 オーストラリア人の友達にベジマイトを初めて勧められる
A (Koa):
Right, it's time. You've been in Australia for three months now, and you still haven't tried Vegemite.
B:
I know, I know. I've been avoiding it, to be honest. I was told it tastes absolutely terrible.
A (Koa):
Whoever told you that was probably eating it wrong. If it were applied correctly, I guarantee you'd at least find it interesting.
B:
How exactly should it be eaten? I've seen videos of people putting huge amounts on toast and gagging.
A (Koa):
That's the classic mistake. Were you to do that, you'd hate it too. The secret is a thin scraping on buttered toast.
B:
Alright, I suppose I should give it a fair go. Had I tried it sooner, I might have gotten used to it by now.
A (Koa):
That's the spirit! Here — I've prepared it for you. Notice how you can barely see it on the toast.
B:
Hmm... it's actually not bad. It's very salty and kind of... savoury? Is that the right word?
A (Koa):
Exactly! It's umami. I wish more people understood that it's meant to be a seasoning, not a spread you pile on.
B:
I can see why Australians love it. If I'd been raised eating this every morning, I'm sure I'd be attached to it too.
A (Koa):
Now you're getting it! Not only is it delicious when used properly, but it's also packed with B vitamins.
B:
Who would have thought? I came in expecting to hate it, and here I am asking for seconds!
