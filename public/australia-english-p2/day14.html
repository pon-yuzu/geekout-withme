Choosing Your Australian City: A Comprehensive Guide

Selecting the right Australian city for a working holiday, study abroad programme, or extended visit is a decision that should not be taken lightly. Each city offers a distinct combination of lifestyle, employment opportunities, and cultural experiences, and what suits one person may not necessarily suit another. If you were someone who thrived in fast-paced, cosmopolitan environments, Sydney would likely be your ideal choice, though it must be acknowledged that the cost of living there is considerably higher than in other Australian cities. Were affordability and a laid-back atmosphere your primary concerns, Brisbane or Perth would be worth serious consideration. Melbourne, often described as Australia's cultural capital, is the destination most frequently recommended for those who value the arts, gastronomy, and intellectual stimulation. For individuals whose priority is proximity to natural wonders, Cairns provides unparalleled access to both the Great Barrier Reef and the Daintree Rainforest. The Gold Coast, meanwhile, appeals to those for whom an active, outdoor lifestyle is essential. It is crucial that prospective visitors research thoroughly before committing, as the city they choose will fundamentally shape their Australian experience.

3 Review
📍 Australia Migration & Education Consultation Centre
⭐⭐⭐⭐

Before visiting this consultation centre in Sydney, I had been overwhelmed by the sheer number of options available for my working holiday. The consultant, a woman named Sarah who appeared to have assisted hundreds of Japanese students over the years, listened patiently to my goals and concerns before offering any advice. She explained that if my primary objective were to improve my English rapidly, I should consider a smaller city where fewer Japanese speakers resided, as this would force me to communicate exclusively in English. Had she simply recommended the most popular cities without considering my individual circumstances, the advice would have been far less valuable. What I found particularly useful was her detailed comparison of living costs across different cities. She reported that Sydney's average weekly rent for a shared room was approximately three hundred dollars, compared to two hundred in Brisbane and one hundred and eighty in Perth. Were I to factor in food, transport, and entertainment, the total monthly difference could exceed one thousand dollars. Sarah concluded by saying that there was no objectively best city — only the city that best matched one's personal priorities and financial situation.

5 🦘 Australia Tips

オーストラリアの都市別生活費の目安

週あたりのシェアルーム家賃の目安: Sydney $280〜350、Melbourne $230〜300、Brisbane $180〜250、Perth $160〜220、Gold Coast $180〜240、Cairns $150〜200。物価は日本の都市部とほぼ同等か若干高め。ただし最低賃金は時給$24.10（2025年時点）と日本より大幅に高いです。

関係代名詞 those who / for whom

'those who value the arts'（芸術を大切にする人々）、'those for whom an active lifestyle is essential'（アクティブな生活が不可欠な人々）のように、those who... や for whom... は準2級で頻出のパターンです。whom は前置詞の後に来る関係代名詞で、フォーマルな文章によく使われます。

6 Conversation
🎭 オーストラリアのワーキングホリデーを考えている友人とオンラインで相談する
A (Koa):
Hey! So I've decided to go to Australia on a working holiday, but I can't figure out which city to go to. Any advice?
B:
That's exciting! Well, it really depends on what's most important to you. If you were mainly interested in the beach and surfing, I'd recommend the Gold Coast.
A (Koa):
I do love the beach, but I'm also keen on finding good part-time work. Which city has the most job opportunities?
B:
Sydney has the most jobs, but the competition is fierce and the living costs are high. Had I been in your position, I probably would have chosen Brisbane — it's more affordable and still has plenty of work.
A (Koa):
What about Melbourne? I've heard the coffee culture is amazing.
B:
Melbourne is fantastic if you were someone who enjoyed arts, food, and a vibrant nightlife. But keep in mind that the winters can be quite cold compared to Brisbane or the Gold Coast.
A (Koa):
Hmm, I really dislike cold weather. What if I wanted to do farm work to extend my visa?
B:
In that case, Perth would be an excellent base. Were you to start from Perth, you'd have easy access to farms in Western Australia, and the pay tends to be higher there.
A (Koa):
I hadn't thought of Perth. Is it true that it's very isolated?
B:
It is, but many people say that's part of its charm. If you had the opportunity to see the sunsets over the Indian Ocean, you'd understand why people fall in love with the place.
A (Koa):
You've given me a lot to think about! I wish I could visit all of them.
B:
Well, if you had a full year, you could actually travel to several cities. Many working holiday makers start in one city and move to others throughout the year.
