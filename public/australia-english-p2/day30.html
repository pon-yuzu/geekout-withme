Reflecting on 30 Days of Australian English

Having completed this thirty-day journey through Australian English, we can now reflect on everything we have learned. Not only have we explored the unique vocabulary and expressions used across Australia, but we have also gained a deeper understanding of the country's culture, geography, and way of life. What we discovered along the way was that Australian English is far more than just slang — it is a reflection of the nation's history, multicultural identity, and laid-back spirit. From the bustling streets of Sydney to the ancient landscapes of the Outback, from the marine wonders of the Great Barrier Reef to the vibrant food markets of Melbourne, each day has introduced us to a different aspect of this fascinating continent. If someone had told us thirty days ago that we would learn so much, we might not have believed them. Whoever said that language learning must be boring was clearly mistaken. Located at the intersection of education and adventure, this workbook has proven that learning can be both rigorous and enjoyable.

5 🦘 Australia Tips

30日間で学んだオージースラング総まとめ

この30日間で登場したオーストラリア独特の表現を振り返りましょう。'G'day'（こんにちは）、'mate'（友達）、'arvo'（午後）、'brekkie'（朝食）、'servo'（ガソスタ）、'reckon'（思う）、'heaps of'（たくさん）、'no worries'（大丈夫）、'fair dinkum'（本物の）、'barbie'（バーベキュー）、'ta'（ありがとう）、'chunder'（嘔吐する）。これらの表現はオーストラリア人との会話で頻繁に使われます。今後もオーストラリアの映画やポッドキャストで耳を慣らしていきましょう！

準2級文法の総復習

30日間で練習した主要な文法事項を確認しましょう。仮定法過去 'If I were...'、仮定法過去完了 'If I had known...'、分詞構文 'Having completed...'、'Located in...'、関係代名詞 what 'What I learned was...'、whoever/whichever、倒置 'Not only...but also'、間接話法 'She said that...'。これらはすべて英検準2級で出題される重要な文法です。日常的に使う練習を続けることで、試験だけでなく実際のコミュニケーションでも自然に使えるようになります。

What I've noticed the most is that I can understand Australian expressions much better now. If someone had used words like 'arvo' or 'brekkie' a month ago, I would have been completely lost.
B:
Fair dinkum! Not only have you learned the lingo, but you've also picked up heaps about our culture, haven't you?
A (Koa):
Absolutely. I learned about Indigenous heritage, the café culture in Melbourne, wildlife conservation, and so much more. Whoever created that workbook did an amazing job.
B:
So what's your plan going forward? If you were to set a new goal, what would it be?
A (Koa):
I wish I could visit Australia this year. Having learned so much about the country, I feel ready to experience it firsthand.
B:
You should definitely come! Whichever city you visit first, you'll feel right at home with your knowledge.
A (Koa):
If I were to choose, I'd start with Melbourne for the food and culture, then head up to Queensland for the reef.
B:
Brilliant plan! Had you not done this workbook, you wouldn't have known about all those hidden gems we talked about.
A (Koa):
That's true. Not only has this workbook improved my English, but it has also given me so much motivation to keep learning.
B:
I reckon you'll smash it, mate. Whatever challenges come your way, you've got the skills to handle them. Cheers to thirty days well spent!
