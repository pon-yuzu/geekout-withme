A Continent of Remarkable Wildlife

Australia is internationally renowned for its extraordinary wildlife, much of which cannot be found anywhere else on the planet. Having been geographically isolated from other continents for approximately 45 million years, Australia has developed an unparalleled collection of endemic species. Marsupials, which carry their young in pouches, represent the most distinctive category of Australian animals. The kangaroo, of which there are over 40 recognised species, has become the nation's most iconic symbol. Equally fascinating are koalas — arboreal marsupials that spend up to 22 hours per day sleeping, sustained almost exclusively by eucalyptus leaves. The platypus, arguably one of the most peculiar creatures ever to have evolved, is a semi-aquatic mammal that lays eggs yet produces milk — a combination so extraordinary that early European scientists initially dismissed specimens as elaborate hoaxes. Rarely does one encounter such biological diversity concentrated within a single continent, making Australia a paradise for wildlife enthusiasts and researchers alike.

3 Review
📍 Taronga Zoo, Sydney
⭐⭐⭐⭐

Situated on the shores of Sydney Harbour with spectacular views of the Opera House and Harbour Bridge, Taronga Zoo is without question one of the finest zoological gardens in the Southern Hemisphere. Having spent an entire day exploring its extensive grounds, I was thoroughly impressed by both the quality of the animal enclosures and the zoo's unwavering commitment to conservation. What I found most captivating was the free-flight bird show, where native Australian birds soared overhead against the stunning backdrop of the harbour. The koala encounter experience, though somewhat pricey at forty-five dollars, was absolutely worth every cent — not only did I get to observe these adorable creatures up close, but I also learned fascinating details about their dietary habits and conservation status. Whoever is interested in Australian wildlife would find this zoo enormously rewarding. The only criticism I would offer is that the zoo is built on a steep hillside, which can be physically demanding for those who are not accustomed to walking uphill for extended periods.

5 🦘 Australia Tips

オーストラリアの固有動物たち

カンガルー（約4,000万頭で人口より多い）、コアラ（1日22時間睡眠）、カモノハシ（卵を産む哺乳類）、ウォンバット（四角いうんちをする）、タスマニアデビル（世界最大の肉食有袋類）など、ユニークな動物が多数います。これらの多くは有袋類で、オーストラリア大陸が長期間他の大陸から隔離されていたために独自に進化しました。

ワーホリ中に動物と出会える場所

野生のカンガルーは郊外や地方では普通に見られます。ただし、夕方〜夜は道路に飛び出してくるので運転注意！動物園ならメルボルン動物園、タロンガ動物園（シドニー）、ローンパイン・コアラ・サンクチュアリ（ブリスベン）が人気。ファームジョブ中に野生のエミューやワラビーに遭遇することもよくあります。

6 Conversation
🎭 ユウキが、クラスメイトのオーストラリア人ミアと動物園訪問の計画について話しています。
A (Koa):
Have you had a chance to visit any of the wildlife parks since you arrived, Yuuki?
B:
Not yet, but I've been keen to go. I've heard that Taronga Zoo is particularly impressive. Would you recommend it?
A (Koa):
Definitely! Not only does it have an incredible collection of native animals, but the harbour views are absolutely stunning as well.
B:
That sounds amazing. I'm especially eager to see koalas. I've read that they're arboreal marsupials that sleep for approximately 22 hours a day.
A (Koa):
That's right! They need all that sleep because eucalyptus leaves provide very little energy. It's fascinating how they've adapted to such a specialised diet.
B:
I'm also curious about the platypus. Isn't it true that it's a mammal which lays eggs? That seems almost impossible.
A (Koa):
It's absolutely real! European scientists who first examined platypus specimens thought they were fake. Rarely does nature produce something so contradictory.
B:
Incredible! By the way, is it possible to hold a koala at the zoo?
A (Koa):
Unfortunately, not in New South Wales. It's only permitted in Queensland. If you were to visit Lone Pine Koala Sanctuary near Brisbane, you'd be able to hold one there.
B:
I see. That's a bit disappointing, but I understand it's for the animals' wellbeing.
A (Koa):
Exactly. Conservation is taken very seriously here. Australia has one of the highest rates of species extinction in the world, so protecting what remains is absolutely essential.
B:
I completely agree. Shall we plan a trip to Taronga this weekend? I'd love to see the free-flight bird show I've read about.
