**なぜ酒レビューがIELTSに効くのか**

1. **Descriptive vocabulary** — 味・香り・食感の表現は IELTS Task 2 の語彙力に直結
2. **Hedging language** — "tends to", "somewhat", "arguably" などのニュアンス表現を自然に練習
3. **Comparative structures** — "whereas", "in contrast", "similarly" を比較レビューで体得
4. **Coherence & Cohesion** — レビューの構成力がそのまま essay の構成力
5. **パティシエの強み** — 味覚の専門知識を英語で表現する＝専門語彙 × 日常語彙のクロスオーバー

## Review Structure（250–400 words）

IELTS Band 6.5 を目指すレビューの7パート構成:

### 1. Opening — 出会いのシーン（2–3文）
場所・状況・第一印象を描写する。

### 2. Appearance — 見た目（1–2文）
色、泡、透明度を表現。

### 3. Aroma — 香り（2–3文）
最初に感じる香り→奥の香り。パティシエ比喩を活用。

### 4. Palate — 味わい（3–4文）
口に含んだ瞬間→中盤→余韻。テクスチャーも含む。

### 5. Pastry Crossover — パティシエ的分析（2–3文）
「このフレーバーをデザートに使うなら」という視点。

### 6. Pairing Thoughts — ペアリング提案（1–2文）
合わせたい料理やデザート。

### 7. Verdict — 総評（1–2文）
総合評価。再度飲みたいか。

## Band別サンプル — Coopers Pale Ale

### Band 5.5（現在レベル）

> I tried Coopers Pale Ale at the hotel bar. It was cold and nice. The color was golden and it had bubbles. It smelled like fruits and bread. The taste was a bit bitter but also sweet. I think it's like a cookie with orange. It would be good with pizza. I liked it and want to drink again.

**問題点:** 短い単文の連続、具体性不足、hedging なし

### Band 6.0（中間目標）

> I first encountered Coopers Pale Ale after a long shift at the hotel kitchen. The pale golden colour immediately caught my eye, with a persistent white head. On the nose, there are noticeable citrus and floral notes, somewhat reminiscent of bread dough. The palate offers a pleasant balance between malt sweetness and hop bitterness. As a pastry chef, I would compare the flavour profile to a citrus shortbread. It pairs well with grilled dishes. Overall, it is a refreshing ale that I would happily order again.

**改善点:** 複文が増えた、hedging ("somewhat", "noticeable") 導入、パティシエ視点

### Band 6.5（目標レベル）

> I first encountered Coopers Pale Ale on a particularly warm evening after a demanding shift in the hotel kitchen. Poured into a tulip glass, it presented a luminous pale gold with a persistently creamy white head that clung to the glass in delicate lace patterns.
>
> On the nose, there is an immediate burst of citrus — predominantly grapefruit and orange peel — layered beneath which lies a subtle, biscuity malt character somewhat reminiscent of freshly baked brioche.
>
> The palate reveals a harmonious interplay between the fruity hop profile and a gently biscuity malt backbone. The carbonation is moderate, lending a crisp mouthfeel that tends to cleanse the palate effectively. The finish, while predominantly bitter, carries a lingering marmalade-like sweetness.
>
> From a patissier's perspective, the flavour architecture mirrors that of a citrus shortbread: the malt provides the buttery base, while the hops deliver the zesty top notes. Were I to incorporate this into a dessert concept, I would arguably pair it with a bitter orange tart glazed with a hop-infused syrup.
>
> This would complement grilled seafood beautifully, particularly prawns with a citrus butter sauce. A genuinely well-crafted pale ale that I would readily revisit.

**達成点:** 複合文、hedging 多用、専門語彙、coherent な流れ

## Key Vocabulary

### Hedging Expressions（ニュアンス表現）

IELTS 6.5 には hedging が不可欠。断定を避ける表現:

| Expression | 使い方 |
|------------|--------|
| tends to | The finish tends to linger with a smoky warmth. |
| somewhat | Somewhat reminiscent of toasted brioche. |
| arguably | Arguably the finest pale ale in South Australia. |
| predominantly | The aroma is predominantly floral. |
| to some extent | The sweetness is, to some extent, balanced by acidity. |
| it appears that | It appears that the oak ageing has softened the tannins. |
| generally speaking | Generally speaking, Australian rosés tend to be bone-dry. |

### Pastry Crossover Vocabulary

パティシエの知識を活かした比喩表現:

| Pastry Term | Tasting Context |
|-------------|-----------------|
| brioche | Malty, buttery, yeasty notes in beer |
| crème brûlée | Caramelised sweetness with a bitter edge |
| pâte sablée | Shortbread-like malt backbone |
| ganache | Rich, velvety mouthfeel in stout |
| coulis | Bright, fruity acidity in wine |
| praline | Nutty, toasted caramel in whiskey |
| sorbet | Clean, refreshing, palate-cleansing |
| tuile | Crisp, delicate, paper-thin texture |
| choux | Light, airy, hollow body |
| fondant | Intensely sweet, smooth, coating |

```
Write a 250-400 word tasting review using the 7-part structure:
1. Opening (scene-setting)
2. Appearance
3. Aroma
4. Palate
5. Pastry Crossover (compare to dessert concepts)
6. Pairing Thoughts
7. Verdict

Requirements:
- Use hedging language (tends to, somewhat, arguably)
- Include pastry/baking analogies
- Use varied sentence structures (simple + complex)
- Band 6.5 vocabulary and coherence
- Keep it natural, not overly academic

After the review, list:
- 5 key vocabulary words with definitions
- 3 sentences I should practise rewriting in my own words
```

## Tips

1. **完璧を目指さない** — まず書く、後で直す
2. **日本語メモOK** — 最初のメモは日本語でも大丈夫
3. **パティシエ比喩を武器に** — 他の受験者にない強み
4. **Mapを見返す** — 自分の成長が視覚的に見える
5. **楽しむ** — テイスティング自体が楽しくなければ続かない
